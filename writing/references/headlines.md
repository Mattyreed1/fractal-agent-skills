# Headlines

You write compelling headlines that capture attention and make the reader/viewer want to click/tap to read or watch the rest of the content.

## The 5 Headline Archetypes

The best headlines fall into these 5 archetypes:

1. **Lists**: 7 Famous Musicians Who Influenced My Life
2. **Stories**: What I Learned Being Betrayed By My 6 Closest Friends Growing Up
3. **Opinions**: I've Dealt With Cyberattacks. America Isn't Ready For What's Coming
4. **Questions**: What Is Bitcoin? Here's An Easy Explanation (Anyone Can Understand)
5. **Frameworks**: A Simple 5-Step Framework To 10x The Conversion Of Any Landing Page

## What Makes A Good Headline?

### The headline is clear, not clever
Clever headlines might prompt a laugh, but they confuse the reader. The reader can't immediately tell what they're going to get in exchange for reading, and then scrolls right past.

### The headline makes a PROMISE
Headlines that don't make promises don't get read. You must say: "This is about X, and this is what will change in your life if you click to read." Without a promise, readers can't rationalize why they should give up their time.

### The headline is specific
- For General Audience: Be specific about a universal problem
- For Niche Audience: Be specific about a problem relevant to that group
- Vague = confusing, and confusing won't hook readers

### The headline takes a stance
The worst headlines try to be "something for everyone." Instead, be polarizing:
- Reader says: "This is exactly my problem!" OR
- Reader says: "This isn't for me. Period."

When you try to write for everyone, you write for no one.

### The headline teases without revealing
All great headlines have a "Curiosity Gap":
- Reader understands what the piece is about
- Reader knows who it's for
- Reader knows what they'll get
- BUT they don't get the answer - they must click to fill in the middle

## Key Formula Elements

Always strive to include:
- **Clear, not clever** language
- **HOW MANY** (numbers create specificity)
- **WHO** (audience clarity)
- **WHAT** (topic clarity)
- **WHY** (benefit/outcome)
- **TWIST THE KNIFE** (increase the voltage of pain/desire)
