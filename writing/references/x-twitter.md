## Proven Single-Sentence Openers

Use single sentence openers for each tweet in a thread. Each new section warrants a new "opener."

If you have 3 main points, use one of these proven openers at the beginning of each main point to re-hook reader attention.

### 1. Open with 1 Strong, Declarative Sentence

**Key**: Don't "hedge."

**Bad Example**: "Some people think cutting costs is the best way to save money. Others believe it's better to just focus on making more. But the truth is, both work."

Horrible! The reader already KNOWS both work. They want YOU to tell THEM which one is correct and why.

**Good Examples**:
- Becoming successful doesn't just "happen."
- The wealthiest people in the world have the simplest investment portfolios.
- Going to the gym isn't a hobby, it's a lifestyle.
- The key to effective project management isn't a tool or platform—it's your ability to listen.
- You can't change the world without first changing yourself.

### 2. Open with a Thought-Provoking Question

What question is your target reader already asking themselves?

Meet them exactly where they are—and they'll feel understood.

**Examples**:
- Did you know the average Millennial has less than $10,000 in their savings account?
- What's the difference between someone who goes pro versus someone who remains an amateur?
- Is there such a thing as eternal happiness?
- Is artistic talent innate? Or can it be taught?
- If you've been thinking about writing online, was your first impulse to start a blog?

### 3. Open with a Controversial Opinion

**Key**: Figure out conventional wisdom in your industry and say the opposite.

**Example**: In online writing, conventional wisdom says "Start a blog." It's "controversial" to say, "Want to start writing online? DON'T START A BLOG."

**Examples**:
- It's impossible to be both rich and happy.
- The U.S. should not be involved in the war between Russia and Ukraine.
- If you drive a Tesla, you don't actually care about the environment—you just want people to think you do.
- Men and women should not make the same amount of money. Women should make more.
- Intelligence isn't something you're born with.

### 4. Open with a Moment in Time

Put a specific date, time, place, age, or price tag in front of your story.

**Compare**:
- "There was a time when I was incredibly insecure."
- "When I was 16 years old, at the height of my popularity in high school, was when I was incredibly insecure."

The specificity pulls you in.

**Examples**:
- In 1982, David Ogilvy wrote an internal memo to the employees of his advertising agency titled "How to write."
- At 5:46 AM Pacific Time, September 11th, 2001, America changed forever.
- My fear of cafeteria trays began in 8th grade.
- When I was 17 years old, I became one of the highest-ranked World of Warcraft players in North America.

### 5. Open with a Vulnerable Statement

Figure out what level of vulnerability works for you.

Vulnerability of any kind pulls readers into your writing.

**Examples**:
- By the time I closed the doors of my first company, I'd lost more than $10 million of investor money.
- I've never been a very social person.
- For 27 years, I've struggled with alcoholism.
- The night before running my first marathon, I called my ex-girlfriend and told her I still loved her.
- For the first 10 years of my career, I was a terrible employee.
- I don't know anything about investing. Nada. Zilch.

### 6. Open with a Weird, Unique Insight

Used all the time by viral documentaries.

Makes you go, "Woah, I didn't know that."

**Examples**:
- Did you know that 85% of the world's oxygen comes from phytoplankton?
- California is not the largest state in the US. Alaska is.
- The average Millennial has less than $60,000 saved up in retirement by the time they reach age 40.
- Crows are one of the most intelligent animals on planet earth.
- My wife can boil a dozen eggs in 7 minutes or less.

## Hook Templates

### 1. The Narrative Flip
Describe a personal transformation related to a trending topic by debunking an old belief.

**Template**:
```
[Trending Topic] is [Old Belief about Trending Topic].

That's what I told myself after [Time Period] of trying and failing at [Trending Topic].

Turns out I was just a [Old Identity].

But after [Time / Money / Spent Or Saved], I figured it out.

Now I [Personal Transformation].

Here's how:
—
```

### 2. The Joke Opinion
Debunk the belief that a trending topic is a joke in a masterclass format.

**Template**:
```
[Trending Topic] is a joke. The best [Sub-Topic] aren't [Misconception].

That's like [Analogy] and [Analogy Misuse].

Here's a 60-second masterclass on how to [Desired Outcome] like a [Ideal Reader Identity]:
—
```

### 3. The I Spent My Weekend Hook
Use a powerful new feature after analyzing it over the weekend.

**Template**:
```
[Trending Topic] can now [Powerful New Feature].

I spent my weekend analyzing the [Benefit of New Capability] with help of the new [Feature].

Here's EXACTLY how to [Desired Outcome] for [Long-Term Result]:
—
```

### 4. The Giveaway Hook
Give users a free solution to a painful problem in a short time.

**Template**:
```
How to use [Tool/Technique] to generate [Huge Outcome] in [Short Timeframe]—even if you think [Common Obstacle or Challenge] (THREAD):
—
```

### 5. Timeless Wisdom
Share key takeaways from an expert's advice.

**Template**:
```
I am obsessed with learning about [Topic].

So I went and devoured [X] years of [Famous Person] advice.

The result?

I walked away with [X] timeless principles from the greatest [Famous Person Status] in the world.

Here are the golden takeaways:
```

## Key Principles for X/Twitter

1. **Hook Hard**: First tweet must stop the scroll
2. **Build Curiosity**: Tease what's coming without revealing it all
3. **Use Numbers**: Specific numbers create credibility and structure
4. **Short Sentences**: Easier to read on mobile
5. **White Space**: Use line breaks generously
6. **Pattern Recognition**: Readers love numbered lists and clear structures
7. **End Strong**: Always include a CTA (follow, repost, sign up, apply)
