# Worked Example: Day In The Life

> Format reference: `references/storytelling.md` § 8.
> Medium: LinkedIn vlog-style post (~170 words).
> Beats hit: Hook → Morning → Core Work → Resolution / Wind Down.

---

A day in the life of a 31-year-old solo founder in Lisbon.

I start every day with cold water and a 20-minute walk because that's the only time my brain works without a Slack tab open. Phone stays in another room until 10am — most people don't know I haven't checked Slack before noon in two years.

Today's biggest priority is a customer interview at 2pm. The challenge: I haven't shipped the feature they asked about three weeks ago, and I have to decide whether to tell them straight or pitch the workaround.

Mid-call, I told them straight. They appreciated it more than the demo I'd prepped — turns out the workaround actually fits their workflow better than the original ask.

End of day: spec drafted for next week, gym at 6, dinner with my partner. Tomorrow I need to send the spec by 11am.

This is what bootstrapped solo-founder life actually looks like. It's not perfect, but it's mine.

---

**Why this works:** identity hook is a specific, scrollable pattern; the "unexpected detail" (no Slack until noon) is the source's flagged secret sauce; the day has a real micro-arc (tension at 2pm, resolution by end of day) instead of a flat to-do list.
