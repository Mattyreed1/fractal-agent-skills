# Worked Example: Challenge

> Format reference: `references/storytelling.md` § 6.
> Medium: LinkedIn recap post (~190 words).
> Beats hit: Hook → Rules/Setup → The Journey (with obstacles) → Resolution.

---

I gave myself 90 days to learn to read music and play one Chopin nocturne. From never having touched a piano. Here's what happened.

The rules: 1 hour every day. No skipping. Video proof every Sunday.

Day 23: I could play hands-separately at quarter-speed. Felt great. Posted my first weekly update and got 40 strangers cheering me on.

Day 41: Left wrist flared up. Lost a full week to ice and Advil. I almost called the whole thing off — couldn't see how I'd make up the gap.

Day 60: Hands together for the first time. Sloppy, but real.

Day 75: I was only 70% there. Bars 47–54 still wouldn't gel. I was averaging 90 minutes of practice instead of 60 just trying to crack them.

Final result: I made it. Day 89, in one take. The last 8 bars are sloppy and I dropped a note in bar 52. But I went from zero to a Chopin nocturne in 89 days.

Picking the next one tomorrow.

---

**Why this works:** bounded mission with rules and a deadline that could fail (the source's distinguishing trait vs. Goal/Dream Journey), specific obstacle moments with stakes, and the result is honest about the rough edges — not a flex piece.
