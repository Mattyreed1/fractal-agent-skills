# Worked Example: Personal Learning / Epiphany (1st Person POV Insight)

> Format reference: `references/storytelling.md` § 2.
> Medium: LinkedIn post (~190 words).
> Beats hit: Hook (Proof) → Backstory → Insight → Breakdown → CTA.

---

I cut LinkedIn ad spend 73% and 3x'd qualified leads in 60 days. Here's exactly what changed.

[Screenshot: $11,400/mo → $3,080/mo. Leads: 17 → 52.]

For a year I was burning budget on LinkedIn's broad audience targeting and tracking CPL like that was the only number that mattered.

The mistake: I was letting LinkedIn pick who to show ads to.

The fix: I built a custom Matched Audience from my CRM and told LinkedIn exactly who to target.

Step 1: Export your top 200 closed-won accounts from your CRM as a CSV.
Step 2: Upload as a Matched Audience in Campaign Manager.
Step 3: Layer 4 specific job titles, not 12. Specificity beats reach.
Step 4: Rewrite the ad headline to name the software they actually use ("Built for HubSpot teams" beats "Built for marketers").

Most teams skip step 4 because it feels too narrow. That's the whole point — the ad self-disqualifies bad leads before you pay for the click.

If you want the full breakdown with my exact ad copy and audience-build screenshots, comment LEARN.

---

**Why this works:** proof-first hook with a screenshot is non-negotiable for this format — without visible proof, the reader has no reason to believe the teaching that follows. The mistake/fix pair is the curiosity hook the source flags as the secret sauce. Numbered steps make the breakdown skimmable.
