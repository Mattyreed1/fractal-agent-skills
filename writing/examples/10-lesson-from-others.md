# Worked Example: Lesson From Others (Mentor Story)

> Format reference: `references/storytelling.md` § 10.
> Medium: LinkedIn post (~170 words).
> Beats hit: Hook → Situation → The Lesson → Application → CTA.

---

I learned something powerful from my old boss Carla.

When I met her she had just lost her biggest client — 60% of revenue, gone in a single Slack DM, with no warning. She had to lay off four people that month.

She told me: "Concentrated revenue is just slow-motion bankruptcy."

Her rule: never let one client be more than 20% of your book. If they cross that line, you're not running a business, you're a contractor with one boss.

When I applied it last year, I turned down a $400K contract that would've put me at 55% concentration. It hurt to walk away — that was three months of expenses.

Six months later, that company pivoted away from agencies entirely and laid off their whole marketing team. If I'd taken the deal, I'd be where Carla was.

If this resonated, save it. You'll need it on a hard day.

---

**Why this works:** Carla is a named, vivid character (the source flags this as the secret sauce). The lesson is a quotable one-liner the reader can repeat. The application is specific enough to be believable — not "I followed her advice" but "I turned down a $400K contract."
