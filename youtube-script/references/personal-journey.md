# Personal Founder-Journey — Format Doctrine

_the user's own lived experience as the vessel for teaching. Memoir + manifesto + how-to. Builds the bond and the reason to subscribe to the user specifically. Slow and expensive to make — space these out. Distilled from the "Founder's Plunge" example._

> **Narrative craft:** read [storytelling.md](./storytelling.md) (Founder Freedom storytelling) before drafting — this format is the FF execution of the "Hero's Journey / Personal Learning" arc, with the six FF narrative moves and the one-metaphor-world rule (Personal Journey is the format that DOES ride a sustained metaphor; Founder Story does not).

## Core promise
"I'll give you the essential mental models you *need* before you make one of the biggest decisions of your life. Plus, the honest, actionable advice I wish someone gave me when I was the one standing on the edge."

**Use it when:** the audience is "frozen at the edge" of a high-stakes life/career decision — curious but afraid to act. Requires a lived transition with a real cost paid. NOT a theory video. Target ~12-15 min.

---

## The arc (a teaching journey on a single metaphor world)

The whole video rides ONE metaphor (Founder's Plunge = cliff → cold water → swim → warm glow). Each teaching Part maps to a stage of the metaphor. Sequence:

1. **Cold-open hook (pre-title).** Direct 2nd-person address to the viewer's pain state. Frame the promise in two sentences. Hand to the visual metaphor (office → cliff → jump → water). _No intro card yet._
2. **Intro narrative / identity statement.** Compressed personal story (the career, the 6-figure job, the success-on-paper, the emptiness, the jump, the exhilaration, the cold reality). End on the open-loop cliffhanger: _"I was finally fulfilled by my work. But, at what cost?"_
3. **[Intro card].** Channel intro plays here.
4. **Open loop / stakes.** Name + credibility drop (AFTER the emotional hook). List the 2-3 burning questions the video answers. Social proof ("two years stumbling through this"). The contract: _"I won't sugar coat it."_
5. **Teaching Parts (the body).** Each Part = an *italic question* → a reframe that dismantles a common objection → specific tactics. Each Part is a stage of the metaphor. From Founder's Plunge:
   - **Part 1 — neutralize the #1 objection.** ("Risky Business": 90% fail, BUT 92% never try → the risk of regret > the risk of failure.)
   - **Part 2 — dismantle the conventional plan.** ("Time to Leap": kill "wait for the right time / side-hustle first" → "Your plan B is someone else's plan A"; "The midwit waits. The founder jumps.")
   - **Part 3 — the resource reality.** ("F*ck This Money": coin the term; define the number — 12+ months runway; give the personal data — 20+ months to cash flow; runway mechanics.)
   - **Part 4 — the emotional core / the cost.** ("The Cold Truth": longest part. "Every sacrifice not made is a competitor's advantage." Then a list of concrete, named tactics. End on the tribe beat: "you aren't alone.")
6. **Resolution / identity shift / CTA.** ("Jump In, the Water's Cold": the metaphor resolves; name the transformation; callback to the opening stat; name who it's for / not for; tease next video; deadpan-warm close.)
7. **CTA card.** Two actions — next video + a tool/quiz.

> Adapt the Part count and names to the topic. The pattern is constant: **hook in their pain → open loop → a sequence of objection-killing reframes, each with tactics, each a stage of one metaphor → resolve the metaphor → turn it on the viewer → point to the next step.**

## Packaging
- **Title pattern:** "The Cold Truth About [The Thing] (leaving your 6-figure job)" — a curiosity-gap noun phrase + a parenthetical that names the concrete situation. Iterate 5 candidates; the parenthetical does the targeting.
- **Thumbnail:** the user's face with the real emotion of the moment (anxious, resolved) + the metaphor visual + a 3-5 word overlay built on a specific counterintuitive number ("92% never even *try*", "The Cold Truth").
- **Hook convention:** lead the packaging with a specific, counterintuitive **number** that implies most people are making a mistake the viewer can avoid.

## Scene / visual conventions
- `[SCENE: ...]` = AI cinematic clip; state camera direction + the emotional function.
- `[DOODLE: ...]` = real-time whiteboard illustration that IS the teaching visual ("Draw 10 tiny startup boats, 9 sinking"; "Draw a 100-person grid where 92 stay frozen").
- Number the metaphor sequence (`s1`-`s8` + `final`); maintain ~12 reusable AI clip archetypes.
- **Teal→orange throughline.** Start trapped/cold (corporate, glass, icy water); end in the warm glow of freedom. Clips 1-8 = core metaphor sequence; 9-12 = body support only when they clarify a teaching point.

## Voice (see [voice.md](./voice.md) for the full doctrine + verbatim samples)
2nd-person pain-first open before the identity drop · short declaratives with mid-thought line breaks · italic-question Part openers · specific vulnerability with real numbers · dry humor + deadpan callbacks · coined terms + contrarian aphorisms · land each Part on a punch before bridging to the next ("But you're probably still wondering...").
