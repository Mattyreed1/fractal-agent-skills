## The three voice pillars (from the brand)

- **Direct.** Every word serves a purpose. No filler, no corporate jargon. If a line isn't load-bearing, cut it.
- **Practical.** Complex ideas simplified into stealable steps. Mental models, not motivation.
- **Honest.** Celebrates the hard road. Names the cost. Rejects quick wins and hype.

Plus the Founder-Freedom-specific layer:
- **Philosophical** — age-old wisdom as modern mental models, derived from lived experience (not academic musings or motivational platitudes).
- **Humorous** — dry wit, self-aware. Takes the work seriously, not themselves.
- **Vulnerable** — the real lows next to the wins. Invites the viewer into the journey.

Governing instruction: **write like a friend having a conversation.** Specifically, like the user talking to himself 2-3 years ago, standing where the viewer stands now.

## Rhythm — how the lines sit on the page

- **Short declarative sentences.** One idea per line.
- **Mid-thought line breaks** for spoken pacing. The break is a breath, a beat, a camera cut.
- **The core pattern:** Setup (2-3 short lines) → pivot (`But` / `Then` / `And then` / `So`) → one-line punch.
- **Land each section on a punch**, then a soft bridge into the next (`But you're probably still wondering...`).
- **Parts open with an *italic question*** the viewer is already asking. The reframe answers it.

Example of the pattern (Personal Journey):
```
A decade long career.
A 6-figure job at a top tech company.
On LinkedIn, I had it all.
But the truth is?
It kinda felt like a prison.
So I left.
```

## Open in the viewer's pain — before the identity drop

Meet the viewer where they are, in 2nd person, BEFORE the user says who they are. The name + credibility come AFTER the emotional hook lands.

```
You're stuck in a cushy but unfulfilling job.
You're wondering what's out there beyond the safety net.
You're feeling the urge to take a leap of faith.
This video is for you.
```

(Founder Story opens differently — on a dramatic contradiction about the founder — but the principle is the same: tension first, throat-clearing never.)

## Specific vulnerability, never vague

Vague vulnerability ("it was hard," "it was a scary time") builds no trust and carries no stakes. Specific vulnerability does both.

- ✅ "Saved enough to buy a house. Invested it into the business instead. Watched the account shrink for 20+ months before consistent cash flow."
- ❌ "It was a financially difficult period."

Show the full trade, not the highlight reel: the loneliness, the dropped relationships, the lost status, the lower income, the slow progress. The brand belief: *no glamorized founder fantasy.*

---

## Dry humor + deadpan callbacks

Wry tactical asides and deadpan callbacks earn the levity. Take the work seriously, not yourself.
- "Now I'm temporarily living at my Dad's, until I move back to Southeast Asia and return to eating multiple mangoes per day for less than $1."
- The closing callback: "Besides, it's really not that cold once you're in." (Pays off the cold-plunge metaphor and the whole video.)

Humor is seasoning, not the meal — roughly 80% education, 20% entertainment.

---

## Coin sticky terms + contrarian aphorisms

the user names things. A coined term or a contrarian one-liner is the most re-clippable, most memorable unit of a video. Aim for at least one per script.
- Coined terms: **"F*ck This money"** (enough runway to leave, not generational "F*ck You money"), **"frozen at the edge,"** **"the cold truth,"** **"founder frugal,"** **"a whole tribe of masochists."**
- Contrarian aphorisms: **"Your plan B is someone else's plan A."** · **"The midwit waits for the perfect time. The founder jumps."** · **"Every sacrifice not made is a competitor's advantage."** · **"Lavish serfdom for lifelong freedom."** · **"Revenue is fuel. Freedom is the destination."**

The aphorism usually IS the punch line that ends a Part.

---

## Reframes with real numbers — and never fabricate

The brand is data-honest ("include sources, don't make unsubstantiated claims"). The signature move is a **specific, counterintuitive number that implies most people are making a mistake the viewer can avoid.**
- "90% of businesses fail. BUT 92% of people who dream about starting one never even try. The risk of failing is *lower* than the risk of regretting your decision not to try."

Every stat must be real and sourced. If you don't have a real number, don't invent one — make the point qualitatively or find the actual figure. A fabricated stat breaks the one thing the brand sells: trust from someone in the arena.

## Belief, not just information

the user's videos carry a point of view. Lean on the **Key Brand Beliefs** when a script needs a spine (use the ones that fit the topic, don't recite them):
- Imposter syndrome is useless — ship anyway.
- Everything you refuse to sacrifice is a competitor's advantage.
- Go all-in once runway is over 12 months; splitting time guarantees slow failure in both.
- Expenses matter more than revenue in year one.
- Sleep + exercise are the highest-leverage activities.
- Freedom is the goal — not a lambo, not status, not followers.
- The best advice comes from someone one step ahead, not ten.

Mantras available when a philosophical beat calls for it: *"Life is beautiful. Life is tragic."* · *"Eigenzeit"* (it will take as long as it must) · *"The way to expand your freedom is to narrow your focus."*

---

## NEVER (anti-AI-slop — these are banned because they're frequent tells)

- "It's not just X, it's Y."
- "Here's why:" as a standalone line.
- "The truth is..." as a flat opener. (the user's rhetorical "But the truth is?" — said like a question, mid-rhythm — is fine. The banned version is the limp AI filler.)
- Generic conclusions: "The bottom line?", "The lesson here?", "At the end of the day."
- **Em dashes for pacing.** the user's spoken style is short lines and periods. Max 1-2 dashes in a whole script; prefer a period or a line break.
- Video throat-clearing: "In this video I'm going to...", "Before we get started...", "Make sure to like and subscribe" as filler. ("Let's dive in" is allowed exactly ONCE, only as the deliberate post-intro-card opener.)
- Hustle jargon, marketing speak, corporate buzzwords, motivational-poster platitudes.

## ALWAYS

- Active voice. Concrete examples. Specific numbers. Real data with sources.
- Short paragraphs / short spoken lines, clear hierarchy.
- Personal stories that connect to a universal lesson.
- Show, don't tell. Earned levity. State the point, then stop.

---

## The read-aloud test (do this before you call a draft done)

Read the script out loud. If a line:
- doesn't sound like the user *talking* → cut or rewrite it,
- needs a breath in the middle → add the line break,
- has an em dash doing pacing work → replace with a period or break,
- makes a claim with a number you can't source → fix the number or drop it,
- could have come from any AI essay → it's slop, replace it with something only the user would say.

## Verbatim calibration samples (the "Founder's Plunge" voice)

**Opening hook (2nd person, pain-first):**
> You're stuck in a cushy but unfulfilling job
> You're wondering what's out there beyond the safety net
> You're feeling the urge to take a leap of faith into entrepreneurship
> This video is for you.
> I'm going to share the cold truth about the founder's plunge.

**Emotional low point (specific, short lines):**
> A decade long career. A 6-figure job at a top tech company.
> On LinkedIn, I had it all.
> But the truth is? It kinda felt like a prison.
> So I left.

**Reframe with a number:**
> 92% of people who dream about starting a business never even try to make it a reality.
> Let that sink in.
> The risk of failing is *lower* than the risk of regretting your decision not to try.

**Transition punch (end of a Part):**
> The time to take the leap is not when you have the income and a clear path to success.
> The time is *now*.

**Tribe / identity beat:**
> As scary as it is to walk away from your comfy corporate life, you aren't alone.
> There's a whole tribe of masochists out here, diving headfirst into the cold unknown.

**Deadpan-warm close (callback resolves the metaphor):**
> There will be glorious ups and unsexy downs. If you want help navigating this adventure, I've got your back.
> Besides, it's really not that cold once you're in.
