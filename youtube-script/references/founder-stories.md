# Founder Story — Format Doctrine

_the user's own format design (captured from Notion KBase). The founder is the vessel, not the point. A Founder Story video = founder biography + philosophy essay + practical playbook in one._

> **Narrative craft:** read [storytelling.md](./storytelling.md) (Founder Freedom storytelling) before drafting — this format is the FF execution of the "Lesson From Others" arc (founder = vessel, viewer = point), with the six FF narrative moves and the real-artifacts visual rule.

> **Before you script (REQUIRED):** run Phase 0.5 Source Gathering — [source-gathering.md](./source-gathering.md). Pull the real footage, quotes, and artifacts into a `sources.md` first. This format is carried by real artifacts, so you must gather them before drafting.

> **Phase 0.75 — render the artifacts:** once the script's `[CLIP: tweet …]` and blog pull-quotes are chosen, use the **`artifact-cards`** skill to turn them into real branded card PNGs (a finished `full` card + a transparent `chrome` card for a DaVinci text type-on). Use the verbatim text from `sources.md` (long-form tweets truncate via the public endpoint — pass the full text). Drops the on-screen assets into the video's `cards/` folder.

## Core thesis
**Famous-or-niche founder story → hidden philosophy → practical founder operating rule → viewer identity shift.** A well-known founder creates built-in search/browse demand; for a lesser-known founder, the contradiction and the lesson create demand instead. Either way, **the non-obvious takeaway earns the watch.**

**Every video answers one question:** _What did this founder understand that most early founders miss?_ Not a biography recap — a useful mental model the viewer can steal.

**Use it when:** a founder's story reveals a useful belief / operating principle / strategic decision; the lesson helps aspiring founders make the leap, survive the early stage, or build a freer life; it maps to an FF pillar (Founder Philosophy, Psychology of Entrepreneurship, Practical Playbooks, Builder's Journey); and there's enough public material for real facts, quotes, and beats.

## 1. Packaging — founder earns the click, lesson earns the watch

**Set Name Recognition (High/Low) BEFORE designing title + thumbnail.**
- **Mode A — high recognition** (Levels, Sivers, Notch, Semler): lead with the face + name. The founder IS the click.
- **Mode B — low recognition** (Chris Walker, Eric Gray, Carolyn Ko): lead with the contradiction or the lesson. The founder is the proof, not the hook. A stranger's face earns no click alone.

**Title formulas:**
- How [Founder] Built [Outcome] by [Counterintuitive Move]
- The Founder Who [Unexpected Action] and Won
- Why [Founder] Refused to [Normal Path]
- [Founder]'s Unfair Advantage Wasn't [Obvious Thing]
- What [Founder] Understood Before Everyone Else

## 2. Thumbnail
Hierarchy: 1) recognizable human face → 2) readable emotional expression → 3) 3-5 word contradiction → 4) one simple supporting visual → 5) title completes the idea. A human face creates emotional friction; a short line creates curiosity; the title carries context. **Avoid thumbnails that make the viewer interpret a metaphor before they care.** Think: _Diary of a CEO clarity + Founder Freedom visual world._ Shoot a real expression — hand-crafted beats AI-rendered (2026 authenticity premium; see retention-and-packaging.md).

Examples: Pieter Levels + product grid → **ONE-PERSON EMPIRE**; Steve Jobs + exile/desert → **THE EXILE YEARS**; Naval + calm face vs chaos → **ESCAPE THE GAME**.

## 3. Intro hook — open on a dramatic contradiction
```
[Founder] should have [done the obvious thing].
Instead, they [counterintuitive action].
And that decision reveals one of the most important lessons in entrepreneurship:
[core lesson].
```
- Bad: "Pieter Levels is a Dutch entrepreneur known for building many startups."
- Good: "Pieter Levels built a startup empire by refusing to build a startup company."

Carry the default path + the contrarian move, compressed: everyone did X, this founder did the opposite, on purpose, and it should have failed. The body pays off how, why, and what it cost.

## 4. Visual storytelling — real artifacts carry it (NOT a metaphor world)
This is the key difference from Personal Journey. A Founder Story is carried by the REAL story, not a cinematic metaphor world. (Full rule in [storytelling.md](./storytelling.md).)
- **Lead with real artifacts:** screenshots, real tweets, live product UIs, interview clips, revenue dashboards. The real artifact is usually the punchline — don't replace it with a symbol.
- **Re-enact, don't symbolize:** where no real footage exists, generated `[SCENE]` clips re-enact what actually happened (coding alone at 3am, the first sale landing), never symbolic metaphor scenes.
- **Ice/fire palette for TONE only:** cold = the lonely grind, uncertainty, sacrifice; warm = earned freedom, clarity.
- **`[DOODLE]` (Tim Urban-style) ONLY for the abstract bits** — diagrams/charts/matrices that explain an idea (e.g. the freedom-vs-scale tradeoff). Diagrams explain; they don't decorate.
- 8-16 total `[SCENE]` tags; consolidate to ~10-15 reusable scenes.

## 5. Open loop — what they'll learn + why it matters now
```
I'm the user, this is Founder Freedom.
If you're [stuck in the job / building the side gig / newly out], this one's for you.
In the next [X] minutes: [Founder]'s story, the philosophy underneath it, and the exact moves you can steal this week.
By the end you'll [core promise / identity shift].
```
Rotate the open every video. Alt doors: **the cost** ("This decision nearly ended him."), **the payoff** ("three moves you can run Monday."), **the mirror** ("Are you building freedom, or a job you own?").

## 6. Story Spine — the four body beats

**Beat 1 — The Story.** Narrative with stakes; the founder as a real character the viewer can picture, not a case study. **THE COST lives here** — show the low point: what the contrarian move risked and cost (loneliness, rejection, lost status, lower income, slow progress, public failure). Don't glamorize. Give the full trade, not the highlight reel.

**Beat 2 — The Philosophy Underneath.** Extract the belief driving the decision. It needn't be one the founder ever stated — name the principle their actions demonstrate. Facts stay real; the interpretation is yours. (If their decisions don't support the read, pick a different founder.) Candidate philosophies: freedom over scale · ownership over status · simplicity over complexity · iteration over planning · distribution over perfection · craft over speed · constraints over optionality · leverage over labor.

**Beat 3 — The Practical Playbook.** Translate the philosophy into specific do-it-this-week moves — the payoff the viewer came for. E.g.: design the business around freedom from day one · use constraints as strategy · outlearn what you can't outspend · ship smaller and listen harder · define "enough" before scale defines it for you.

**Beat 4 — The Identity Shift.** Turn the lesson on the viewer; land on a high note (motivated to move, not just informed).
```
So the question is not: "How do I become [Founder]?"
The question is: "What did [Founder] understand that I'm currently ignoring?"
```
Then 2-4 direct questions (building for freedom or recreating a job you own? chasing scale for the mission or because it sounds impressive? constraints as weakness or the strategy you should've chosen?). Return to the through-line and land the belief:
```
In the end, [Founder] didn't win because [obvious reason].
They won because [deeper reason].
And that's the lesson: [final distilled belief].
```

## 7. CTA
Connect the lesson to the next FF asset: next video in the founder journey · Stay-Split-Commit quiz · runway calculator · Founder Frugal / burn-bot tools · soft Fractal AI CTA only when automation naturally fits the topic.


**One-line positioning:** Extract a philosophy from a successful founder, turn it into practical advice, and leave the viewer with an identity shift that motivates them to move.
