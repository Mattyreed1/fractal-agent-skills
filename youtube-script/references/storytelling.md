# Founder Freedom Storytelling — Narrative Doctrine

_How the user tells a story on Founder Freedom. This is the craft layer between `voice.md` (how the lines sound) and the format references (the exact beats). Distilled from the "Founder's Plunge" script, the Founder Stories design, and the brand voice. It is FF-specific on purpose — it replaces generic storytelling theory for this channel._

The brand promise governs every story: **"Real stories and advice from someone two steps ahead, not ten."** A Founder Freedom video is a lesson worth stealing, carried by a true story, told by someone close enough to the struggle to be trusted. Roughly 80% teaching, 20% story-craft and levity. If a viewer can't retell the lesson to a friend afterward, the story failed.

> **Theory root (optional, skip-able):** the two FF formats specialize Kallaway's "Hero's Journey / Personal Learning" (Personal Journey) and "Lesson From Others" (Founder Story). The generic, medium-agnostic versions live in `writing/references/storytelling.md`. You do not need them — draft from the FF doctrine below; it overrides the generic execution for this channel.

---

## The two FF story shapes

- **Personal Journey = "I crossed this, here's what it taught me."** the user is the hero; the viewer is the next hero, standing where he stood. Carried by ONE metaphor world (e.g. the cold plunge). → beats in [personal-journey.md](./personal-journey.md).
- **Founder Story = "they did the un-obvious thing, here's the belief you can steal."** The founder is the vessel; the *viewer* is the point. Carried by REAL artifacts, not metaphor. → beats in [founder-stories.md](./founder-stories.md).

---

## The six FF narrative moves (what makes a story sound like Founder Freedom)

### 1. Cost-forward honesty (the signature move)
Show the cost. Specifically, early, and never glamorized. The cost is what makes the lesson credible and the channel trustworthy — it is the opposite of highlight-reel founder content. *"Every sacrifice not made is a competitor's advantage."* Real numbers, real trades: the shrinking bank account, the 20+ months to cash flow, the dropped relationships, the loneliness of building alone. Vague cost ("it was hard") builds nothing. If a story has no cost, it has no stakes, and it isn't an FF story.

### 2. But / Therefore, never "and then"
Connect every beat by conflict or consequence. In FF stories the BUTs are usually the cost (what the move risked); the THEREFOREs are the earned insight. _"He had a 6-figure job. BUT it felt like a prison. THEREFORE he left. BUT the account started shrinking..."_ Bullet the events, force a BUT or THEREFORE between each pair, cut anything that's only "and then." (Re-hook cadence + open-loop stacking live in [retention-and-packaging.md](./retention-and-packaging.md).)

### 3. Extract a philosophy, don't recap a biography
Especially for Founder Stories. The founder isn't the point; the stealable belief is. Name the principle their actions demonstrate (it needn't be one they ever stated out loud). Facts stay real; the interpretation is the user's. The governing question: **what did this person understand that most early founders miss?** If their decisions don't support the belief, pick a different subject.

### 4. Reframe to dissolve the objection
the user's core teaching motion: take the viewer's objection or the conventional wisdom, then flip it with a specific counterintuitive number or a contrarian aphorism. _"90% of startups fail. BUT 92% never even try. The risk of regret is higher than the risk of failure."_ · _"Your plan B is someone else's plan A."_ Each Part of a Personal Journey is one of these reframes; each Founder Story philosophy is one too. The number must be real — data-honesty is non-negotiable (see voice.md).

### 5. One thesis, one throughline
A single stealable sentence governs the whole video; every beat advances or deepens it. The throughline is concrete: Personal Journey rides ONE metaphor world (cold plunge: cliff → cold water → swim → warm glow); Founder Story rides the real-artifact spine + the philosophy. Never two theses. Never a metaphor that competes with the lesson.

### 6. Turn it on the viewer (the identity shift)
End by making the lesson theirs. 2-4 direct mirror questions ("Are you building freedom, or a job you own?"), then land on a motivating high note — the viewer moved to act, not just informed. _"The question isn't how do I become them. It's: what did they understand that I'm ignoring?"_

## The visual-storytelling choice (the user's explicit rule)

The two formats tell their story through DIFFERENT visual logic. Don't mix them up:

- **Personal Journey → a metaphor world carries it.** One sustained metaphor (the cold plunge), teal→orange throughline. `[DOODLE]`s teach the frameworks. The metaphor IS the architecture.
- **Founder Story → real artifacts carry it.** Screenshots, real tweets, live product UIs, interview clips. Where no real footage exists, generated clips *re-enact what actually happened* (coding alone at 3am, the first sale landing) — never symbolic metaphor scenes. Ice/fire palette for tone only (cold = the lonely grind, warm = earned freedom). `[DOODLE]`s (Tim-Urban style) ONLY for the abstract bits (e.g. the freedom-vs-scale tradeoff).
- **The rule:** generated clips depict the real story; diagrams explain the idea; sustained metaphor worlds are for Personal Journey only.

---

## Belief, not just information
Every FF story carries a point of view. Lean on the **Key Brand Beliefs** (in voice.md) for spine when a story needs one — freedom over scale, expenses over revenue in year one, go all-in past 12 months of runway, sleep + exercise as highest-leverage. A story that informs but takes no stance isn't Founder Freedom.

---

## The story test (before a draft is done)
- Is there a real character with real stakes?
- Is THE COST shown — specific, early, unglamorized?
- Does every beat connect by but/therefore (no "and then")?
- Is there exactly ONE thesis and ONE throughline?
- Founder Story: did I extract a philosophy, or just recap a life?
- Does it turn on the viewer and land on a high note?
- Would a viewer retell this to a friend tomorrow?
