# Design Patterns Catalog

Proven layout patterns organized by slide type. Select patterns during Phase 1 planning. Mix and match within a carousel — variety keeps attention.

---

## Carousel Slide Types

### TITLE SLIDE (Slide 1 — The Hook)
**Purpose**: Stop the scroll. One bold statement.

**Layout A — Centered Statement**
- Large headline centered vertically and horizontally (64-80px)
- Subtitle below (28-32px, muted color)
- Social handle or author name at bottom (20px)
- Optional: accent color bar or geometric shape as visual anchor

**Layout B — Split**
- Left 60%: headline text, large and bold
- Right 40%: accent color block, geometric pattern, or subtle image
- Handle at bottom spanning full width

**Layout C — Full Bleed**
- Background: bold accent color or dark
- Single large headline (80px+), centered
- Minimal — just the words and space

---

### STAT SLIDE (Data Point)
**Purpose**: One number that makes them think.

**Layout A — Hero Number**
- Single massive number centered (120-160px, bold)
- Label below the number (28-32px)
- One-sentence context below that (24px, muted)
- Optional: thin horizontal line separating number from context

**Layout B — Before/After**
- Two numbers side by side or stacked
- "Before" in muted color, "After" in accent color
- Arrow or separator between them
- Context sentence below

**Layout C — Number + Icon**
- Large number on left (120px)
- Descriptive icon or simple illustration on right
- Label and context below

---

### LIST SLIDE (Multiple Points)
**Purpose**: Deliver 3-5 actionable insights.

**Layout A — Numbered List**
- Large number for each item (36-48px, accent color)
- Item text next to number (24-28px)
- 3-5 items max per slide
- Consistent spacing between items (30-40px)

**Layout B — Icon List**
- Simple emoji or icon for each item (left-aligned)
- Item text next to icon (24-28px)
- Items separated by thin lines or generous spacing

**Layout C — Two-Column**
- Items split across two columns (for 4-6 items)
- Each item: bold keyword + one-line explanation
- Works best for comparison or category lists

---

### QUOTE SLIDE
**Purpose**: Borrow credibility or emotional resonance.

**Layout A — Centered Quote**
- Large opening quotation mark (accent color, 80-120px)
- Quote text centered (32-40px, italic or serif font)
- Attribution below in smaller text (20-24px)
- Generous whitespace above and below

**Layout B — Pull Quote**
- Thick accent-color bar on left side
- Quote text left-aligned next to bar (28-36px)
- Attribution right-aligned below

---

### PROCESS/STEP SLIDE
**Purpose**: Show a sequence or workflow.

**Layout A — Vertical Steps**
- Steps listed top to bottom
- Each step: number (accent) + title (bold) + one line description
- Thin connecting line or arrow between steps
- 3-4 steps per slide max

**Layout B — Horizontal Flow**
- Steps left to right (works in landscape formats)
- Each step: icon/number above, label below
- Arrow connectors between steps
- Best for 3 steps

---

### CTA SLIDE (Final Slide)
**Purpose**: Tell them what to do next.

**Layout A — Simple CTA**
- Action-oriented headline (48-64px): "Follow for more" / "Save this for later" / "DM me 'AI' to get started"
- Social handle prominently displayed (32-40px)
- Optional: profile picture or logo
- Clean, uncluttered — this slide should feel like a landing page

**Layout B — Summary + CTA**
- Brief recap of key takeaway (24-28px, 1-2 lines)
- Bold CTA below (40-56px)
- Handle and optional URL at bottom

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER do these)

- **Wall of text**: More than 6 lines of body text per slide
- **Tiny fonts**: Anything below 18px on social media
- **Rainbow colors**: More than 3-4 colors per design
- **Centered everything**: Mix alignment for visual interest
- **No hierarchy**: Everything the same size/weight
- **Clip art**: Generic icons or stock illustrations that look templated
- **Drop shadows on text**: Unless on a photo background with proper contrast
- **Gradient overload**: One subtle gradient is fine; gradients on everything is not

## Aesthetic Directions

When the user doesn't specify a style, choose one of these and commit fully:

| Direction | Characteristics | Best For |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **Minimal** | Lots of whitespace, thin fonts, muted palette, clean lines | Professional/B2B content |
| **Bold** | High contrast, thick fonts, saturated colors, strong shapes | Attention-grabbing stats |
| **Editorial** | Serif fonts, elegant spacing, magazine-like, sophisticated | Thought leadership |
| **Technical** | Mono fonts, dark background, code-like elements, grid-heavy | Developer/tech audience |
| **Playful** | Rounded shapes, warm colors, hand-drawn elements, casual | Creative/consumer audience |
